Sawdust City • Gravenhurst

Twin Oaks

$7.92

A barrel aged Belgian IPA blended with 35% fresh west coast IIPA…. but this isn’t about the hops! Aged for a year in oak barrels, the hops have faded but the funk has INVADED! Pouring a brilliant, deep gold, there’s lots of oak and brett aromas on the nose. Fruity, chardonnay like flavours greet you through the middle and a faint reminiscence of hops lingers like a memory on the tongue. The finish is dry, with a lively acidity that helps with the drinkability. A rich, honey-like mouthfeel brought from the fresh IIPA also helps to contribute a nice fuller, more round body to the beer as well. Can conditioned and aged for 7 months in the can before we released it, this big beer is meant for slow sippin’ and deep contemplation. Plus it’s north of 10%, so proceed cautiously.

Sam's Take on Twin Pines:

In the past we’ve not blended our barrel aged Princess with anything and released it as ODB. We always really liked that brand, but as our pallets changed, we felt it could use a bit more body to help fill out the mouthfeel and make it slightly less astringent. So this year we took 35% fresh Twin Pines IIPA and blended it with barrels of Princess that had been aging for over a year. We felt the big oily mouthfeel of the west coast IIPA would give us the extra mouthfeel we were looking for. The results are fantastic. The extra body helps make the beer much more drinkable while also bringing a richer, more gold honey colour to the beer. The hops have mellowed, but they blend wonderfully with the fruit forward brett character of the barrel aged Princess.
