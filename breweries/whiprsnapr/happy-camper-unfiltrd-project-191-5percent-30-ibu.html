Brewer’s take.

Yes – we’ve done a happy camper before, but this is a brand new beer and a new location on the label for the happy campers 😉
This year, we put a little hazy twist on it and used exclusively Simcoe hops.
 
Simcoe hops are described as:  Simcoe at 12-14% AA has great bittering qualities, but also packs a complex aroma of stone fruit, pine, and citrus zest. A dual purpose hop that is capable of standing on its own in single-hopped beers in a wide range of styles.

 
So we did just that – single hopped it, but focused more on the flavour/aroma profile than the bittering properties and I really love he way it’s turned out.  It’s really more of a "lightened up" version of the Rock’n’Hops NEIPA’s we do. More approachable and ultimately more refreshing, it balances the concepts of Hazy and an American Pale Ale. Loaded with hop goodness on the nose and the palate, it brings a slightly juicy smoothness that is accentuated by a balanced hop bitterness.  Happy Camper delivers a more easy drinking beer, with great aromatics and fantastic flavour that will let you go back for another… and another…
 
Specifically, I get ripe peach on the nose, with mango and tangerine also quite prominent.  Flavour is more delicate, easier, with the hops not overwhelming, no hop burn and very little perceived bitterness.  You do get those delicious ripened stone fruit flavours and citrus notes.
