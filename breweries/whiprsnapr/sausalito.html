Sausalito West Coast IPA

6%  487 IBU

Sausalito, California. Just across the Golden Gate Bridge from San Francisco. It’s known for its houseboat enclaves, and just a beautiful little town.

Perfect place to enjoy an incredible West Coast IPA.  Featuring Cascade and Mosaic hops.

 
Ian’s take:

I’ve always loved west coast IPA’s, so I’m going to continue pushing the agenda 😉

Sara and I have done a little travel to California (not enough for either of our liking…) and this west coast series will continue, and feature a number of small towns and locations that we’ve visited, or want to visit.

Sausalito is a cute little town just over the Golden Gate bridge from SF.  We took a little tour through and had the most amazing fish tacos at ‘FISH’ (aptly named).  Right on the fishermans docks, just fantastic.

 

But on to the beer…

West Coast IPA’s are best known for their clean bitter bite and hop forward flavours and aromatics.  They’re perfect for enjoying with just about anything – fish tacos, pizza, steak, bbq… as it helps cuts through the flavours of the food, and cleanses the pallet for the next bite. 

For Sausalito, we kept the bittering at a milder 48 IBU’s, and had the % come in at reasonable 6%.  Both provide a good level of flavour, and balanced bitterness.  A little on the malty side, but not too far and dried out with a little cane sugar.

Hops.  We used west coast classic Cascade hops in the boil, then dry-hopped with Mosaic.  The result is a tasty balance of citrus, mango and pine with aromas of tropical and stone fruit.  A little east meets west, while still residing comfortably in California.
