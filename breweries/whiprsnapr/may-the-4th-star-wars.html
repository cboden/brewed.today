May the 4th Star Wars

$20.00 + tax

Unfiltrd Project #254. PDW’N 4% Dry Hopped Blonde.

Ahsoka is a padawan in the series, and thus the lightest of the bunch at just 4%.

Floral, citrusy hop flavour and malt characteristics of baked bread and nose of fresh grain shine through.

Light and super easy drinking, a solid way to start of the set.

Willamette and East Kent Golding hops. Willamette for classic blonde and dry hopped with EKG for floral nose and subtle citrus flavour.

Unfiltrd Project #255. Knights 5.7% NEIPA.

Obi-Wan and Anakin are the Jedi Knights heroes in this series and are well represented in this bright and juicy NEIPA.

Fruit salad is how I’d best describe the juicy fruit flavours in this tasty NEIPA.

Callista and Citra hops. Callista really brings out the fruit salad aspect, and citra lays down a nice base of bright citrus. Callista is a new to us hop from Germany, and we’re quite happy with it as an alternative to American NEIPA hops

Unfiltrd Project #256. Night Sister 6.2% Cascadian IPA.

The Night Sister is one of the baddies of the series, and I think she’s just a bad@ss – so went with her as the bad guy of the series.

Cascadian Dark IPA is a style we haven’t done exactly before, but similar with Troublmakr and other black IPA’s.

This one isn’t quite black, but still quite dark for the ‘dark side’! Fantastic roasted notes, like cold iced coffee with a hint of caramel sweetness.

Centennial hops provide a nice contrast of punch grapefruit citrus that really makes this beer pop. (might be my fav…..)

Unfiltrd Project #257. Master 8.2% DIPA.

Yoda is a Jedi Master, and fitting as the heavy hitter of the pack.

Thick, super dank and incredibly juicy. If you close your eyes, it may remind you of his home swamp planet of Dagobah. 😉

Tango, Mosaic and Idaho 7 hops bring a well balanced array of flavours. Idaho 7 hits with the sticky dank, Mosaic with the tropical/stone fruit and Tango delivers a big hit of passion fruit.

Tango is another new to us hop that again comes from Germany. Looks like another great NEIPA alternative to the US ones!
