In most modern breweries, fermentation happens in closed environments to keep out bacteria and prevent off-flavours. But with spontaneous fermentation beers like Intermodal, we invite wild yeast and bacteria from the air.
Each fall, during a narrow window of specific cool overnight temperatures (10-15C), we fill our open-air coolship (which is a broad, open vessel) with hot wort and let it rest and cool overnight. During this time, wild yeast and microorganisms floating in the air are introduced to the beer, rather than us adding a lab yeast.
Next, we transfer the wort into neutral, steam-sanitized and hand-scrubbed barrels that have no living organisms left in the wood, where it sits for a long time.
